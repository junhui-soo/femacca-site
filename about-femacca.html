About FEMACCA

Since the late 19th century, the Chinese from China have been going to the South China Sea and taking root in Southeast Asia, from the early miners to businessmen, the Chinese also set up businesses in the region, making the region a thriving and prosperous place for various industries. "to promote mutual contact, protect the rights and interests of the industry, and establish schools and schools for social welfare. The mission is to benefit Chinese people.

On August 27, 1954, the Registrar of Societies approved the registration of the "General Association of Selangor Chinese Trade Associations", which has since played an important role in the fields of politics, economics, culture and education, and was renamed the "General Association of Selangor Chinese Trade Associations" in 1987. In 1987, the name was changed to the The Federation of Malaysia Chinese Guilds Associations (FEMACCA). In addition to the founding objectives of FEMACCA, the mission of FEMACCA is to promote goodwill and unity among all ethnic groups and to liaise with groups that are in line with the mission of FEMACCA.

2003

The Association was reorganized as a national association and renamed as The Federation of Malaysia Chinese Guilds Associations (FEMACCA).

2013

The 1st Malaysian Enterprise Golden Scholar Award is organized by the General Chamber of Commerce to recognize the outstanding contributions of elites in the relevant industries, and to inspire industry players and other entrepreneurs with their success stories, inspiring them to invest in creativity and transformation, driving the development of the domestic industry, and also setting an example for future generations, from which to implement the The spirit of "taking the lead and inspiring the next".

7 February 2015

The "2nd Malaysia Enterprise Golden Scholar Award Ceremony" was successfully held and the "Golden Scholar Award" has become a brand event of FEMACCA, which has been unanimously recognized by the central government, the business community, associations and the media, and has established FEMACCA position as the top of the industry.

On June 28, 2015, the reform team, which advocates transformation and advancing with the times, in order for FEMACGA to go "international" and open up a bigger market for the domestic industry, FEMACGA has transformed itself again by launching a new logo and changing its name to "Federation of Malaysian Chinese Industries "("FEMACGA" for short). FEMACGA hopes to reshape its image, expand its influence and strengthen its representation through the name change, and move forward towards the international development pattern of "based in Malaysia and looking overseas".

After establishing the route of going international, FEMACGA also organized a delegation to Guangzhou, China in 2015 to visit the headquarters of Huawei, a technology giant, and later organized another delegation to Zhongshan and Huizhou, China, and visited the headquarters of WeChat, so that the delegation members could absorb the latest international technology information and understand the development of related industries abroad. development.

In addition, Hsin Chong has been actively involved in the construction of various industries in China, especially in providing opinions on government policies and criticizing decisions that are detrimental to the development of the industry. We also participate in the meetings of the central government to collect budget proposals, reflecting the needs of various industries and providing suggestions. We have also been appointed as an advisor to the Malaysian Small and Medium Enterprises Development Centre (SAME) under the Prime Minister's Department, and are also the recommender and vetter for the RM100 million loan from the central government for Chinese SMEs.

In 2016, SAME will continue its efforts by deploying overseas study missions to learn from overseas, as well as organizing the 3rd Malaysian Enterprise Golden Scholar Award, and in line with SAME's internationalization strategy, SAME will also organize the At the same time, in line with the internationalization of Hsin Kuang, the company also organized the "1st Malaysian Brand Award" to recognize the successful Chinese brands, so that they can further gain a foothold in Malaysia and open the way to the international market.

Words from President

The Federation of Malaysian Chinese Industries (FEMACCA) is the highest representative of all Chinese industries in the country, belonging to more than 117 members who are organizations and groups from all walks of life, and is the umbrella of Chinese industries in the country.
As the highest representative of all Chinese industries, the General Chamber of Commerce has the obligation to ensure the steady growth of all industries in China and make the greatest contribution to the country's economic and social development. Therefore, Hsin Chong is determined to lead the transformation and change of all industries in China, so that it can keep pace with the times and stay at the cutting edge of technology, and avoid the relevant industries from being eliminated in the flood of the development of the times.
The transformation and change of Hsin Kuang also starts from itself, which includes leading two overseas study trips for the council members in 2015, including the successful IT companies of today, namely Huawei and Wechat headquarters, to let the council members understand the new IT trends. At the same time, in order to give greater recognition to various industries in China, especially through the sharing of experiences and struggles of successful entrepreneurs, which will serve as a model for other entrepreneurs and bring positive effects to the development of the industry, the Executive Committee also organized the first Golden Scholar Award for Malaysian enterprises in the end of 2013 and will continue to organize the second Golden Scholar Award in 2014. Through the awards, the entrepreneurs are given recognition and praise, which injects positive energy into the industry and helps to promote the growth of the industry.
Through the awards, and in conjunction with SAME's various programs, including business matching, venture capital investment, and corporate finance and loans, we encourage innovation and change in the domestic industry so that it can continue to stand firm in the domestic market and move further into the international arena.

"We welcome more industry associations and groups to join the FEMACCa and hope to move forward hand in hand with all industries as we head towards our 100th anniversary."
